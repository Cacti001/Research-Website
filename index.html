About Me

I am Raima Sing, a recent graduate with an Integrated MSc (2019-24) in Life Sciences from the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER), Bhubaneswar, India. My Master's thesis focused on investigating the role of cold-sensitive TRP ion channels in microglial function and iron homeostasis. I am currently working as a project student investigating how TRP channels regulate microglial iron homeostasis and its possible role in neurodegeneration. I aspire to conduct transformative research that improves the lives of individuals affected by chronic disorders while advancing societal health and well-being.
